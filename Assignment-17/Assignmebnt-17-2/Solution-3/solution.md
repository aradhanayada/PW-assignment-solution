## Unary, Binary, and Ternary Operators in JavaScript

### Unary Operators:
- Operate on a single operand.
- Example: `++x`, `--x`, `!condition`.

### Binary Operators:
- Operate on two operands.
- Example: `x + y`, `a * b`, `x === y`.

### Ternary Operator:
- Operates on three operands.
- Example: `condition ? expr1 : expr2`.
